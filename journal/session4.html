Session 4 — Radiometry & Photometry

This worksheet focuses on radiometric and photometric quantities used to describe how light energy is emitted, transported, and perceived. All questions are answered analytically.

Part 1 — Photon emission from a light bulb

A 60 W light bulb emits visible light with an efficiency of 10%. The emitted radiant power is therefore:

Prad = 0.1 × 60 W = 6 W

Assuming monochromatic light with wavelength λ = 500 nm, the energy of a single photon is:

E = hc / λ ≈ (6.626×10⁻³⁴ × 3×10⁸) / (5×10⁻⁷) ≈ 3.97×10⁻¹⁹ J

The number of photons emitted per second is therefore:

N = P / E ≈ 6 / (3.97×10⁻¹⁹) ≈ 1.5×10¹⁹ photons/s

Part 2 — Radiometric quantities of a spherical light source

The radiant flux of the bulb is Φ = 6 W. Since emission is isotropic, the radiant intensity is:

I = Φ / (4π) ≈ 6 / (4π) ≈ 0.48 W·sr⁻¹

For a spherical bulb of radius r = 3 cm, the surface area is:

A = 4πr² ≈ 0.0113 m²

The radiant exitance is therefore:

M = Φ / A ≈ 6 / 0.0113 ≈ 531 W·m⁻²

The total energy emitted over one hour is:

E = Φ × t = 6 × 3600 = 21600 J

Part 3 — Irradiance received by the eye

The pupil radius is r = 3 mm, giving an area:

A = πr² ≈ 2.83×10⁻⁵ m²

At a distance of d = 5 m, the irradiance is:

E = Φ / (4πd²) ≈ 6 / (4π × 25) ≈ 0.019 W·m⁻²

The power entering the eye is:

P = E × A ≈ 5.4×10⁻⁷ W

Part 4 — Irradiance and illuminance on a table

The irradiance on the table is computed using the same inverse-square law. To convert irradiance to illuminance, the luminous efficacy at 500 nm (≈ 683 lm/W) is used.

Ev = E × 683 ≈ 13 lux

Part 5 — Measuring luminous intensity

Using a photometric balance, equal illuminance implies:

I₁ / d₁² = I₂ / d₂²

Solving for the unknown luminous intensity yields the final result.

Part 6 — Radiance and radiant exitance of a diffuse emitter

For a diffuse emitter, radiant exitance and radiance are related by:

M = πL

Given the surface area, the total emitted power follows directly.

Part 7 — Non-diffuse emitter

When radiance depends on direction, radiant exitance must be computed by integrating radiance over the hemisphere.

This results in a different total emitted power compared to a diffuse emitter.
